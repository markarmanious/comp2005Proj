Management Structure, Processes and Tools.

  • Management Structure

    To insure smooth running and coordination of the project we decided to have a structured group with two team leaders. A communications leaders who is responsible for all external and internal communications e.g. representing the group to the user and coordinating communication among group members as well as meetings. A technical leader who is there to provide technical guidance and ensure the same technical standards are being used through out the project.

    Jared Pope - Communications Leader.
    Mark Armanious - Technical Leader.

    Isaac Murisa - group member.

  • Meetings and Communication Strategies

    Due to tight personal schedules we resolved to have our meetings weekly on Monday, Wednesday and Friday from 3.000ppm - 5.30pm(+). Meetings were mandatory and each group member was expected to attend. Despite communications through email we also had a SLACK workspace for the group. Group discussions and or informal communications during the week and weekends were communicated through the workspace. We also created a git repository for the project were all the files and resources were shared.

  • Tools Some of the tools used by the group were:
    • Slack - Used for group communication and sharing of resources.
    • Git - Used to store and share project files. Allow development and collaboration on project files.

Team Work assignments

Regular Meeting: These meetings are going to be held every week and the project workflow and documentation will be discussed. Discussions will include; requirements gathering, development, feedbacks and brainstorming.
Delivarables: combined testing and pip zip file: After everyone is done testing and developing their modules and tasks, the team will group and combine all the modules designs into one final project. The final project will be tested, and a runnable pip installation zip file is to be created.
